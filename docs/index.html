My name is Allen McIntosh II, a JavaScript Developer. I'm from The Bahamas, I'm 24 years old, I like playing basketball to stave off becoming a circle, and I also enjoy exercising my brain with the various headaches problems that programming provides—for those who don't already know, it's fun (we swear).

Compared to other people I've seen who have been developing for years on end, some even from childhood (you lucky bastards), I cannot say the same in the slightest. When I was first exposed to programming, I was already (legally) "a grown adult"—that was back in university, circa 2014. It didn't take long after Computer Programming II (a bunch of slideshows and easy multiple choice quizzes) to realize that maybe I chose poorly in how I would go about becoming the next Shigeru Miyamoto, and I ended up leaving school shortly after that.

It's now 2016 in this story, and I've officially started my journey as an autodidact; I fiddled with Ruby and the Rails framework for all of 2–3 months, but ultimately settled on JavaScript. Besides, it was the same language on the frontend and the backend, and was simple enough to setup—in short, it's noob newb friendly and it gets the job done to this day so I haven't had a real need beyond curiosity to switch things up. There's still PLENTY to learn from this one community alone—even ignoring the fact that like Pokemon, there's much too many packages out in the wild now for anybody to actually blame somebody for not knowing all of them, there's nonetheless many concepts, technologies, and paradigms for me to wrap my head around.

I live to improve, and I strive to be as proficient as I can be at what I choose to work with. It's a lot of work, but the constant evolving is what made me want to become a programmer in the first place—I'll manage.
